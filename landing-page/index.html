What Glass is -- and what it is not

If you are evaluating Glass for your organization, here is what you need to know upfront.

Glass IS
An AI observability layer that logs every operation, checks claims for internal consistency, and produces tamper-evident proof bundles.
Glass is NOT
A replacement for ChatGPT, Copilot, or any other AI product. Glass wraps your existing AI and makes its actions auditable.
Glass IS
A tool that exports PDF and JSON proof bundles with signature blocks, control references, and verification instructions your auditor can use.
Glass is NOT
A fact-checker. It checks whether the AI's claims are internally consistent with its reasoning -- not whether they are true in the external world.
Glass IS
Open source software your developers install and run. Free. No vendor lock-in. No telemetry. Data stays on your infrastructure.
Glass is NOT
A SaaS platform or managed service. It runs on your hardware, using your LLM backend (local Ollama, or cloud providers like Anthropic, OpenRouter, or Azure OpenAI).

How it works

Glass runs your query through a six-stage pipeline. Every stage is visible as it happens:

Generate -- An LLM produces a response with step-by-step reasoning.
Decompose -- The response is broken into atomic, checkable claims.
Check -- Each claim is assessed for internal consistency. (This is a coherence check, not a fact-check.)
Audit -- Every operation is logged: calls made, destinations, timing, bytes moved.
Seal -- A tamper-evident hash chain is computed over the audit trail.
Export -- Download a proof bundle as JSON (machine-verifiable) or PDF (for legal and compliance teams).

Core principles

Honest by structure

Consistency checking is architectural, not prompt-engineered. A separate checker assesses every claim against the reasoning that produced it.

Process-first

The audit trail is the primary interface -- always visible, never hidden behind a toggle.

Local-first

Runs on your hardware via Ollama. Cloud is opt-in with visible data flow indicators. No telemetry.

Portable proof
