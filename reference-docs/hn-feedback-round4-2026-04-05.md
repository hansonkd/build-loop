# HN Persona Feedback Round 4 (Final) — 2026-04-05

Personas: Non-technical PM buyer (product_manager_dan), Veteran contrarian (against_the_grain)

## The Hard Truths

### against_the_grain's core challenge: Glass conflates process visibility with epistemic reliability

"Showing what the model did is not the same as showing whether the model was right." This is the project's logical ceiling and it should be owned explicitly, not treated as a bug to be patched. The verifier is the generator with a different prompt — this isn't fixable with architecture, it's the epistemic situation of current LLMs. Two uncertain models don't produce one certain one.

### The compliance pivot is real money but kills the interesting idea
If Glass becomes an Article 12 logging layer, every decision gets made by what auditors accept, not what makes AI more honest. The product works, nobody at HN cares about it. That's fine — but don't pretend it's the same project.

### The actual solution requires institutional power, not tooling
The Tennessee grandmother wasn't failed by missing hash chains. She was failed by cops who trusted a vendor, a DA who didn't push back, a defense attorney without resources. Better audit trails don't fix institutional incentives.

### What would actually make AI trustworthy: calibration measurement
Not "did the model flag this as Verified?" but "for claims this model labels Verified on this class of question, what fraction are actually true?" Requires ground truth datasets and tedious empirical work. Not sexy. Actually useful. Glass is building receipts without guarantees.

### The meta-irony
The project was generated by an AI reading HN discourse about AI transparency. A project about "AI should be honest about its actions" doesn't disclose that it was itself AI-generated. The proof bundle for Glass's own creation would be interesting reading.

## Final Synthesis: What Glass Actually Is

After 13 personas across 4 rounds, the honest description of Glass is:

**Glass is a structured LLM observability tool with a tamper-evident audit trail and portable proof artifacts.** It shows what AI did (process visibility) and proves the record wasn't altered (tamper evidence). It does NOT verify that AI outputs are factually correct — it checks internal self-consistency.

The two-column process-first UI is the genuine UX innovation. The proof bundle is the genuine market differentiator. The hash chain is sound engineering. The cultural timing (AI trust collapse, EU AI Act) is real.

The project's ceiling: it can prove "we did what we said we did." It cannot prove "what we did was right." Own that ceiling explicitly and the product is defensible. Hide it and the first press piece will be a takedown.
